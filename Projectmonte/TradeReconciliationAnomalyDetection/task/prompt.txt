Analyze trading data across four input files to identify anomalies and data quality issues that indicate operational problems or potential fraud. The files are market_data.csv with daily OHLCV prices, signals.csv with trading signals including limit_price and validity windows in valid_from and valid_until columns, executed_trades.csv with actual trade executions including signal_id references and execution time, and position_reports.csv with periodic position snapshots showing reported_position for each symbol and report_date.
Detect six categories of anomalies. UNMATCHED_TRADE anomalies are trades whose signal_id is NONE, REVERSAL, or references a signal not present in signals.csv because unauthorized trades bypass risk controls and audit trails. DUPLICATE_TRADE anomalies flag the subsequent occurrence when two trades share identical date, symbol, side, quantity, and price because duplicates indicate booking errors or system glitches. TIMING_VIOLATION anomalies are trades executed outside their signal's valid_from to valid_until window because out-of-window execution suggests stale signals or manual override and this applies only to trades with valid signal references. PRICE_DEVIATION anomalies are trades where execution price differs from signal limit_price by more than 3 percent relative to limit_price because significant slippage indicates poor execution or market manipulation and this applies only to trades with valid signal references. POSITION_MISMATCH anomalies occur when cumulative net position differs from reported_position by more than 100 shares where net position accumulates from all trades up to report_date with buys adding and sells subtracting starting from zero because discrepancies indicate reconciliation failures or missing trades. SAME_DAY_REVERSAL anomalies occur when a buy and sell of the same quantity exist for the same symbol on the same date because offsetting trades may indicate wash trading and at most one reversal per symbol per date should be reported.
Severity reflects risk exposure. HIGH severity indicates large financial impact from price deviations exceeding 10 percent, position mismatches exceeding 500 shares, unmatched trades exceeding 1000 shares, or timing violations more than 2 hours outside the window. MEDIUM severity indicates elevated risk from pre-market timing violations, all duplicates, and all same-day reversals. LOW severity applies to remaining anomalies with contained risk.
Output anomaly_report.csv with columns anomaly_type, identifier, date, symbol, details, severity ordered by date then anomaly_type. The identifier is the trade_id for trade anomalies, SYMBOL_DATE format for position mismatches, or comma-separated trade_ids for reversals. Output anomaly_summary.csv with columns metric and value including total_anomalies, total_affected_value as the sum of quantity times price for anomalous trades, affected_symbols as comma-separated alphabetically sorted symbols, count per anomaly type as count_TYPE, and count per severity as severity_LEVEL.
