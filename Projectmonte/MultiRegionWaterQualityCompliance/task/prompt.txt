I need a water quality compliance analysis for 30 monitoring stations across five regions for January through June 2024. Read all input files from the task directory.
Clean each station's readings in chronological order before computing. For ph, turbidity, and chlorine, carry forward the last valid measurement per station, defaulting to the caution threshold when none exists. Null lead becomes zero. Null coliform rows are excluded only from the coliform average. Null flow rate marks the row as offline and excludes it from all quality calculations. readings_count is the total row count; valid_readings_count counts non-null-flow rows.
For each online reading compute a Pollution Deviation Index per parameter against the caution, violation, and critical limits in thresholds.csv. At or below caution the score is zero. Between caution and violation it scales linearly from 0 to 0.5 across that interval. Between violation and critical it scales from 0.5 to 1.0 across that interval. Above critical it is 1.0. Average those per-reading scores by parameter for the month, then form a Composite Quality Index as 100 times one minus the weighted mean using weights 0.30 lead, 0.20 turbidity, 0.20 coliform, 0.15 ph, 0.15 chlorine. Clamp to 0 through 100 and round to 2 decimal places.
Keep a Rolling Compliance Score per station in date order. The first data month sets it to the CQI. For subsequent data months: CQI at or above 80 blends as 0.85 times prior plus 0.15 times CQI; CQI between 60 and 80 blends as 0.60 times prior plus 0.40 times CQI; CQI below 60 replaces the score outright. A month with no data decays the score by multiplying by 0.95. Round to 3 decimal places after each update. For the risk trend, compare each data month CQI to the prior data month CQI: IMPROVING if gain exceeds 2, DECLINING if drop exceeds 2, STABLE otherwise, NEW for the first data month, INSUFFICIENT for no-data months.
Classify each station month by checking these conditions in priority order: CRITICAL_REPEAT when the highest single-reading PDI exceeds 0.9 and a FAIL inspection falls within the 90 days ending on the last day of the month inclusive; CRITICAL when peak PDI exceeds 0.9; MULTI_VIOLATION when three or more parameter averages exceed 0.5; HEALTH_HAZARD when both lead and coliform averages exceed 0.5; SINGLE_VIOLATION when any average exceeds 0.5; WATCH when any average is positive; COMPLIANT otherwise. No-data months are NO_DATA. Assign numeric tiers 5, 4, 3, 3, 2, 1, 0 in that sequence with NO_DATA also at 0. For tier 2 or above, consecutive_violations counts the unbroken stretch of immediately prior months also at tier 2 or above, not counting the current month; tier below 2 uses zero.
For months with tier above zero, gross penalty equals the base amount from penalty_rates (matched by month start date inside the effective date range) times the adjustment factor from regional_adjustments for that region and quarter (default 1.0 when absent) times the workday fraction times one plus 0.15 times consecutive_violations, rounded to 2 decimal places. Workday fraction is weekdays in the month minus holidays from holidays.csv matching the station region or ALL, divided by calendar days, rounded to 4 decimal places. Maintenance credit is the smaller of 5 percent of total parts plus labor from maintenance_log for that station and month or 20 percent of gross penalty, rounded to 2 decimal places. Net penalty is the larger of zero and gross minus credit.
