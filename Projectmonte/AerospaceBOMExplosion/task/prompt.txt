Generate aerospace manufacturing analysis for 12 assemblies with multi-level bill of materials containing sub-assemblies and modules. Read all CSV input files from the task directory. The exploded_requirements output must have one row per leaf component showing total quantities summed across all assembly demand sources.
For exploded_requirements.csv, recursively traverse the BOM tree starting from each assembly demand quantity. At each parent-child relationship, multiply the parent quantity by quantity_per adjusted for scrap using qty times one plus scrap_rate, treating null scrap as zero. Sub-assemblies prefixed SUB or MOD have their own children that must be recursively exploded until reaching leaf components. Sum total requirements when the same component appears from multiple paths. Include component_id, total_required_qty rounded to 2 decimals, num_parent_assemblies counting distinct assembly sources, deepest_level showing max BOM level encountered, category, unit_cost, and extended_cost as total times unit cost. Sort by component_id.
For material_shortages.csv, compare each component total requirement against available inventory calculated as on_hand minus allocated. Gross shortage equals max of zero and required minus available. Net shortage equals max of zero and gross shortage minus on_order quantity. Only include components with gross shortage above zero. For substitution analysis, find APPROVED substitutes ordered by lowest priority_rank that have available inventory above zero. Calculate substitute quantity needed as gross shortage times conversion_factor and check if substitute available inventory covers it. Output component_id, required_qty, available_qty, on_order_qty, gross_shortage, net_shortage, is_critical, substitute_id, substitute_factor, substitute_available, and substitute_can_cover as YES or PARTIAL or NO. Sort by component_id.
For purchase_orders.csv, group components with net shortage above zero by their default supplier. Order quantity is max of net shortage and min order quantity, rounded to whole units. Apply tiered pricing based on quantity ordered. Check if total value meets supplier minimum. Output po_number in PO00001 format, supplier_id, num_line_items, total_qty, total_value, max_lead_time_days using the supplier base lead time, meets_minimum as YES or NO, and min_order_value. Sort by supplier_id.
For critical_path_analysis.csv, compute total lead time by traversing the BOM tree recursively. Leaf components use their lead_time_days. Sub-assemblies add processing time of 5 days for SUB prefix, 10 days for ASM prefix, or 7 days otherwise to the max of their children lead times. Identify the critical path child with max lead time. Buffer is 14 days for CRITICAL priority or 7 days otherwise. Output assembly_id, priority, due_date, total_lead_time_days, num_direct_children, critical_path_child, critical_child_lead_time, buffer_days, and required_start_days as lead time plus buffer. Sort by assembly_id.
For cost_rollup.csv, compute bottom-up cost accumulation through the BOM. Leaf components use unit_cost as material, labor equals labor_rate_per_hour times run_hours_per_unit from category rates, and overhead equals labor times overhead_rate minus one. Sub-assemblies accumulate child total costs times quantity_per times one plus scrap_rate, add fixed labor of 25 for SUB or 75 for ASM or 10 otherwise, and overhead at 50 percent of labor. Multiply unit costs by demand quantity. Apply 15 percent margin. Output assembly_id, demand_quantity, unit_material_cost, unit_labor_cost, unit_overhead_cost, unit_total_cost, extended_material, extended_labor, extended_overhead, extended_total, margin_rate, margin_amount, and selling_price. Sort by assembly_id. Round all monetary values to 2 decimal places.
