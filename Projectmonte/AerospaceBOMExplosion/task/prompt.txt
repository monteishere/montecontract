You are an aerospace manufacturing analyst preparing a production readiness report for the upcoming build cycle, covering material availability, supply chain risk, and total build cost. Read all input files from the task directory. The analysis date is June 1, 2024. Round monetary values and quantities to four decimal places unless stated.

Write supplier_scorecard.csv with one row per supplier showing total deliveries, on-time deliveries, and a reliability score equal to on-time divided by total rounded to four decimal places. The consecutive late count is the length of the unbroken late streak at the end of the delivery record, inclusive — if the most recent delivery was late it contributes, and the count is zero when the last delivery was on time. Include the average delay in calendar days across late deliveries only, rounded to two decimal places and zero when none were late. Sort by supplier ID.

Write exploded_requirements.csv showing the total compounded component demand for each assembly. Each row covers one assembly-component pair, with gross quantity required representing full demand incorporating scrap losses that compound at every level of the bill of materials at a rate of quantity_per times one plus the scrap rate per edge through sub-assembly and module levels to leaf components. Net quantity required is gross minus available inventory (on-hand minus allocated, floor zero). Unit price is the applicable tier at the gross quantity required. The contract discount is the rate in the supplier contracts file matching the component's default supplier and category — when that combination is absent, or when every matching entry falls outside June 1, 2024 (effective-from on or before and effective-to on or after), the discount is zero. Discounted unit price is unit price times one minus the discount. Sort by assembly ID then component ID.

Write material_shortages.csv for every row where net quantity required is greater than zero. The available substitute quantity is the on-hand stock of any approved substitute for that component. Shortage covered is YES when available substitute stock meets or exceeds the net quantity required and NO otherwise. Shortage severity is CRITICAL when the component is flagged as critical or the net quantity required exceeds fifty, and STANDARD otherwise. Sort by assembly ID then component ID.

Write purchase_orders.csv for each shortage where covered is NO. Quantity ordered is the ceiling of the larger of net quantity required and minimum order quantity. Unit price is from the tiered table at the quantity ordered and purchase order value is quantity ordered times that price. The expedite fee is capped at the supplier's maximum — it equals the lesser of three percent of the purchase order value and that cap. Adjusted lead time reflects delivery risk from recent supplier history and equals the ceiling of base lead time multiplied by one plus 0.1 times the supplier's consecutive late count, so longer streaks produce larger lead time buffers. Reliability adjusted is YES when that count is above zero and NO otherwise. Sort by assembly ID then component ID.

Write cost_rollup.csv with one row per assembly. The capacity multiplier comes from only the sheet named operational_schedule in the production schedule spreadsheet — match the assembly's due date month, preferring the row where priority scope exactly matches the assembly's priority, falling back to the row with priority scope ALL for that month, or 1.0 when no row qualifies. Material cost is the sum of gross quantity required times unit price across all leaf components, and discounted material cost is the same sum using discounted unit price instead. Effective material cost is discounted material cost times the capacity multiplier. Labor cost sums gross quantity required times run hours per unit times labor rate per hour times overhead rate by component category. Total cost is effective material cost plus labor cost. Sort by assembly ID.
