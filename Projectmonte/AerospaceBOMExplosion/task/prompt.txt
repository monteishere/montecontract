You are an aerospace manufacturing analyst preparing a production readiness report. Read all input files from the task directory. The analysis date is June 1, 2024. Round monetary values and quantities to four decimal places unless stated otherwise.

Write supplier_scorecard.csv by processing each supplier's delivery history in chronological order. For each supplier record the total delivery count, on-time count, and a reliability score equal to on-time divided by total rounded to four decimal places. The consecutive late count is the number of trailing late deliveries at the end of the sequence counted inclusively, so a final late delivery contributes to the streak and the count is zero when the last delivery was on time. Include the average delay in calendar days for late deliveries only rounded to two decimal places and zero when none are late. Sort by supplier ID.

Write exploded_requirements.csv by recursively traversing each assembly's bill of materials. At every edge multiply the parent demand by quantity per unit and by one plus the scrap rate, descend into sub-assembly and module children until reaching leaf components, and accumulate per assembly-component pair. Include demand quantity, gross quantity required, and net quantity required where net equals gross minus available inventory (on-hand minus allocated, floor zero). Choose unit price from the tiered pricing table at the gross quantity required. For contract discount look up the supplier contracts file by default supplier and then by category. When the combination is absent, or when it exists but no entry has an effective-from on or before and effective-to on or after June 1, 2024, the discount is zero. Otherwise apply that entry's discount rate. Discounted unit price is unit price times one minus the contract discount. Sort by assembly ID then component ID.

Write material_shortages.csv for every row where net quantity required is greater than zero. The available substitute quantity is the on-hand stock of any approved substitute without netting. Shortage covered is YES when that meets or exceeds the net quantity required and NO otherwise. Shortage severity is CRITICAL when the component is flagged as critical or the net quantity required exceeds fifty, and STANDARD otherwise. Sort by assembly ID then component ID.

Write purchase_orders.csv with one row per shortage where shortage covered is NO. The quantity ordered is the ceiling of the larger of net quantity required and minimum order quantity. Unit price comes from the tiered pricing table at that quantity. Purchase order value is quantity ordered times unit price. The expedite fee is the smaller of three percent of the purchase order value and the supplier's maximum expedite fee cap. The base lead time is the component's standard lead time. Take the consecutive late count for the default supplier from the scorecard and compute adjusted lead time as the ceiling of base lead time multiplied by one plus 0.1 times that count. Reliability adjusted is YES when the consecutive late count is greater than zero and NO otherwise. Sort by assembly ID then component ID.

Write cost_rollup.csv with one row per assembly. For the capacity multiplier read only the sheet named operational_schedule from the production schedule spreadsheet, match the due date month in YYYY-MM format, and find the row where priority scope matches the assembly's priority exactly. If no exact match exists for that month use the row with priority scope ALL, and if neither exists use 1.0. Material cost is the sum of gross quantity required times unit price across all leaf components, and discounted material cost is the same sum using discounted unit price. Effective material cost is discounted material cost times the capacity multiplier. Labor cost sums gross quantity required times run hours per unit times labor rate per hour times overhead rate by component category. Total cost is effective material cost plus labor cost. Sort by assembly ID.
