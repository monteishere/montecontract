Generate aerospace manufacturing analysis for 12 assemblies with multi-level bill of materials containing sub-assemblies and modules. Read all CSV input files from the task directory. The exploded_requirements output must have one row per leaf component showing total quantities summed across all assembly demand sources.
For exploded_requirements.csv, recursively traverse the BOM tree starting from each assembly demand quantity. At each parent-child relationship, first compute adjusted_quantity as quantity_per times (1 plus scrap_rate), treating null scrap as zero, then multiply adjusted_quantity by the parent quantity, and round the resulting edge quantity to 2 decimal places before accumulating across paths. Sub-assemblies prefixed SUB or MOD have their own children that must be recursively exploded until reaching leaf components. Sum total requirements when the same component appears from multiple paths. Include component_id, total_required_qty rounded to 2 decimal places, num_parent_assemblies counting distinct assembly sources, deepest_level showing max BOM level encountered, category, unit_cost, and extended_cost as total times unit cost. Sort by component_id.
For material_shortages.csv, compare each component total requirement against available inventory calculated as on_hand minus allocated. Gross shortage equals max of zero and required minus available. Net shortage equals max of zero and gross shortage minus on_order quantity. Only include components with gross shortage above zero. For substitution analysis, find APPROVED substitutes ordered by lowest priority_rank that have available inventory above zero. Calculate substitute quantity needed as gross shortage times conversion_factor and check if substitute available inventory covers it. Process components in component_id ascending order when evaluating substitutes. Maintain a shared substitute inventory pool initialized from each substitute component's available inventory (on_hand minus allocated). When a substitute is assigned to cover a shortage, deduct the substitute quantity used from that substitute's pool balance immediately so subsequent components in the same run see the reduced remaining pool. The substitute_available column reports the pool balance at the moment of assignment. When no approved substitute with available pool balance above zero exists, output empty for substitute_id, 0.0 for substitute_factor, 0 for substitute_available, and NO for substitute_can_cover. Output component_id, required_qty, available_qty, on_order_qty, gross_shortage, net_shortage, is_critical, substitute_id, substitute_factor, substitute_available, and substitute_can_cover as YES or PARTIAL or NO. Sort by component_id.
For purchase_orders.csv, group components with net shortage above zero by their default supplier. Order quantity is the smallest whole multiple of min_order_qty that is greater than or equal to net_shortage. Apply tiered pricing based on quantity ordered to get unit price and compute line total as quantity times unit price rounded to 2 decimals. Check if total value meets supplier minimum. Output po_number in PO00001 format, supplier_id, num_line_items, total_qty, total_value, max_lead_time_days using the supplier base lead time, meets_minimum as YES or NO, and min_order_value. Sort by supplier_id.
For critical_path_analysis.csv, compute total lead time by traversing the BOM tree recursively. Leaf components use their lead_time_days. Sub-assemblies add processing time of 5 days for SUB prefix, 10 days for ASM prefix, or 7 days otherwise to the max of their children lead times. Identify the critical path child with max lead time. Buffer is 14 days for CRITICAL priority or 7 days otherwise. Also compute capacity_adjusted_days as the ceiling of required_start_days divided by the capacity_multiplier for the assembly due_date month found in production_calendar, where required_start_days is lead time plus buffer. Compute required_start_date as due_date minus capacity_adjusted_days calendar days formatted as YYYY-MM-DD. Output assembly_id, priority, due_date, total_lead_time_days, num_direct_children, critical_path_child, critical_child_lead_time, buffer_days, required_start_days, capacity_adjusted_days, and required_start_date. Sort by assembly_id.
For cost_rollup.csv, compute bottom-up cost accumulation through the BOM. Leaf components use unit_cost as material, labor_cost equals labor_rate_per_hour times run_hours_per_unit rounded to 2 decimal places, and overhead_cost equals the rounded labor_cost times (overhead_rate minus one) rounded to 2 decimal places. Sub-assemblies accumulate each child unit_total_cost times quantity_per times one plus scrap_rate, rounded to 2 decimal places per child contribution before adding to the running total, into the parent unit_material_cost, so child total cost rolls up entirely as parent material. Sub-assemblies then add fixed labor of 25 for SUB or 75 for ASM or 10 otherwise, and overhead at 50 percent of labor. Multiply unit costs by demand quantity to get extended values. Margin is calculated on extended_total, so margin_amount equals extended_total times 0.15 rounded to 2 decimals, and selling_price equals extended_total plus margin_amount rounded to 2 decimals. Output assembly_id, demand_quantity, unit_material_cost, unit_labor_cost, unit_overhead_cost, unit_total_cost, extended_material, extended_labor, extended_overhead, extended_total, margin_rate, margin_amount, and selling_price. Sort by assembly_id. Round all monetary values to 2 decimal places.
